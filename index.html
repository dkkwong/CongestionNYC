Congestion Pricing in New York City

By: David Kwong

What is Congestion Pricing?


Under New York's congestion pricing plan drivers will have to pay a toll to drive through lower Manhattan. The price of the toll will be higher during daytime and lower at night.


Drivers will only be charged once per day for entering or staying within the congestion zone.


The plan is estimated to generate 1 Billion for the MTA. 80% will go for subways and buses, 10% to the LIRR, and 10% to the Metro North.


Congestion pricing in New York is currently planned to be implemented mid 2023

source: MTA

Why does New York City need it?


A golbal traffic survey by INRIX found that New York City had the worst traffic on the continent in 2021.


Before COVID the MTA estimated average traffic speeds within the proposed congestion zone were just 7MPH.


This traffic results in millions of hours lost for New Yorkers and over $8 Billion in cost for the city, which doesn't include damages from traffic accidents or the increased air pollution.


New York City's plan is based on sucessful congestion pricing plans in London, Singapore, Stockholm, and Milan. These cities saw ~25% drop in traffic after their plans were enacted.

source: INRIX 2021 traffic report

Who will be affected?


Residents making less than $60,000 who live in the congestion zone will be refunded any extra cost in the form of a state tax credit.


Most commuters do not drive to Lower Manhattan.The MTA estimates 75%-80% of commuters currently use alternative forms of transportation.


All New Yorkers will benefit from reduced air pollution, traffic noise, and increased speeds.
